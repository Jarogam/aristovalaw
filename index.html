Tatiana S. Aristova, Esq. concentrates her practice in business and employment-based immigration, with particular emphasis on National Interest Waiver cases. Ms. Aristova represents clients before the US CIS nationwide, serving clients located in the US and abroad. Ms. Aristova also litigates immigration cases and appeals in federal courts.

Ms. Aristova received her Juris Doctor degree from Temple University in Philadelphia, PA (2000), where she had the honor of being selected as an editor at Temple Law Review (1999-2000). She also holds MBA degree from New York University (2011). Ms. Aristova received her B.A. and M.A. degrees in Structural and Applied Linguistics from the Moscow State University (MGU) in Moscow, Russia (1992).

Ms. Aristova is a member of the American Immigration Lawyers Association (AILA). Ms. Aristova is admitted to practice in the following courts and jurisdictions: PA Supreme Court (2000); NJ Supreme Court (2000); US District Court for the Eastern District of PA (2000); US District Court for the District of NJ (2001); US District Court for the Eastern District of NY (2000); US District Court for the Southern District of NY (2000); US Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit (2000); US Court of Appeals or the Second Circuit (2003); US Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit (2002); US Court of Appeal for the Ninth Circuit (2003).

Effective Handling Of Challenging Immigration Matters

Ms. Aristova is known for taking on challenging immigration cases that other attorneys turn away because she has extensive knowledge in immigration law and because she deeply understands what’s at stake and how much it means to immigrants to achieve their dreams.

No matter how simple or complex the situation may seem, Ms. Aristova will make sure you feel supported, informed and confident about your next steps. She will use her decades of experience to evaluate the situation and provide you with the best advise tailored to your specific situation.

Trusted Guidance From Someone Who Knows The Process Well

Ms. Aristova has personal experience with the immigration process because as an immigrant, she went through the process herself. This experience gives her insights into the pitfalls and difficulties most immigrants face as well as the tight timelines some immigrants have to face with their immigration status. For clients who are seeking work-related visas, she applies her training from her MBA program at New York University to build a strong business case for their immigration to America.

She will personally advise you on document collection and application submission deadlines as well as help prepare you for your immigration interview. Your calls and emails will always be addressed in a timely manner, so you feel connected and up to date on things in real time.
